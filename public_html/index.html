Portfolio

Developed an algorithm for national vaccination strategy

My student team got personally approached by the Dutch municipal and regional health service (GGD GHOR Nederland) after winning the national competition with a university project. We were asked to support the Dutch vaccination campaign by developing an optimization algorithm, leading to improved vaccine distribution in the Netherlands.

The created methodology has been operationally used since June 2021 in order to more effectively distribute vaccines across the many GGD injection sites, hastening the immunization of the Dutch population. Additionally, the new strategy has increased the level of parity in immunization rates across the various GGD zones. Future vaccination efforts are one of the follow-up measures that are now being thought about. For instance, utilizing mathematical models to better locate puncture sites is being considered. What was merely a university project in January has evolved into a nationwide solution that improves public health in the Netherlands: "The sky is the limit."

Note: Due to confidentiality, the details of the project cannot be posted online.

Other team members: Berend Markhorst, Nina Malbasic, Renze Dijkstra, Daan Otto

Winning the national competition for logistic innovation COVID-19 vaccination strategy

The objective was to build an integrated logistical and creative solution for the long-term planning of a pandemic vaccination campaign that could vaccine 95 per cent of the Dutch population promptly, thoughtfully, responsibly, and properly. The submission to the competition was done in collaboration with other student teams from HAN University of Applied Sciences and Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. The final product won the first prize (10.000 euros) of a national competition for logistic innovation COVID-19 vaccination strategy in the TKI Dinalog challenge. Afterwards, a paper on the topic was published.

Check out extra references in the TKI DINALOG article or Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam article .

Publishing a peer-reviewed article at MDPI Journal

Together with Berend Markhorst, Nina Malbasic, Renze Dijkstra, Daan Otto, Rob van der Mei and Dennis Moeke, we have published an article "A Data-Driven Digital Application to Enhance the Capacity Planning of the COVID-19 Vaccination Process" in the MDPI Journal special issue Vaccination Strategies for COVID-19.

In this paper, a decision support system (DSS) is presented that focuses on the capacity planning of the COVID-19 vaccination process in the Netherlands. As the user is given the freedom to experiment with different starting positions and strategies, the DSS is ideally suited for providing support in the dynamic environment of the COVID-19 vaccination process. In addition to the DSS, a mathematical model to support the assignment of inhabitants to medical hubs is presented. This model has been satisfactorily implemented in practice in close collaboration with the Dutch Municipal and Regional Health Service (GGD GHOR Nederland).

Bachelor thesis

The bachelor thesis of Business Analytics was done on a business study in collaboration with Vrije University Medical Center (VUmc). The study investgated the impact on bed capacity after reorganisation of patient groups at the cardiology department of the VUmc. For this reason, two deliverables were created: 1) A performance dashboard that serves a decision support system for VUmc's management to make well-founded decisions regarding the capacity of their hospital wards, and 2) The time-dependent simulation model that serves as a performance measure and experimental tool.

Other team members: Lotte Meijs, Mees Benninga, Hatim Akidas, Shushan Hachnazaryan.
